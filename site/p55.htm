The Des Moines Tribune of Saturday, November 17th, published the following account of the accidental death of Frank Anderson, a well known former resident of Prairie City. Frank Anderson, fifty-one year old farmer, was found dead under his wagon on the Carbondale road a mile east of Youngstown at 7:30 a.m. today. The wagon, heavily loaded with cement, with the team hitched still, was standing at the bottom of a steep hill. The horses were heavily coated with frost; Mrs. Frank Moran, who lives near there, saw them and went to see who was with the horses. She found Mr. Anderson under the rear axle of the wagon dead. Coroner Guy Clift was called to conduct an inquest. The death is believed to have been caused by a fractured skull received either in a fall from the wagon to the ground or a kick by one of the horses. Mrs. Moran said today that the wagon was heavily loaded and the team was unable to pull it up the hill. Mr. Anderson, she said, probably went to the rear of the wagon to help by pushing, slipped and fell, fracturing his skull. The horses were unable to leave the spot because of the heavy load they were hitched to.
  7. [S36] Obituary of Frank Early Anderson. Frank Early Anderson was born January 19, 1873, east of Prairie City on the old home place and died Friday evening, November 16, 1923, aged fifty years, nine months and twenty seven days. His father, Geo. F. Anderson, died the latter part of December, 1921 and his mother, Mary Ellen Young Anderson, died in May, 1914. With his family Frank moved from the farm east of Prairie City in September, 1918. In this vicinity he lived, labored, and died -- and his life's history will stand to show a good honorable citizen. He was married to Mary Ellen Gilbert February 26, 1896. To this union three children were born, Mary Elizabeth, Lylas Ethel and Gilbert Freeman. The wife and three children survive him to bear the sad stroke of grief of his sudden departure. He joined the Prairie City order of IOOF No. 144 when he was twenty-one, and remained a staunch believer and true worker throughout his entire life. The life journey is ended. He left in composure and peace. "God's finger touched him and he slept," but to awaken in a more beautiful and brighter realm. Funeral services were conducted at the Christian church at Prairie City Monday afternoon at 2:30 by the pastor, Ivan J. Young, assisted by Rev. Strawn, of Altoona. Interment was in Waveland cemetery beside his father and mother. The IOOF order gave their beautiful ritualistic service at the grave. CARD OF THANKS: To all who have helped us in the hour of sorrow and expressed their sympathy and kindly appreciation....

He was a fond father to her daughter Edith, who resided in the home with them.
