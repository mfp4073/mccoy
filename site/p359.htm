Robert Lind Douglass

M, b. 18 March 1814, d. 28 November 1877
     Robert Lind Douglass was born on 18 March 1814 at Hagerstown, Washington Co., Maryland.1 He was the son of Robert Douglass and Sarah Coombs. Robert Lind Douglass married Sophia Elizabeth Hastings, daughter of Albert Merwin Hastings and Frances Berry, on 26 May 1847.1 Robert Lind Douglass died on 28 November 1877.1 Note.2

Child of Robert Lind Douglass and Sophia Elizabeth Hastings

  • Charles Hastings Douglass3 b. 16 Dec 1849, d. 27 Feb 1855

Citations

  1. [S207] Francis H. Hastings, Family Record of Dr. Seth Hastings Senior of Clinton New York, page 141.
  2. [S207] Francis H. Hastings, Family Record of Dr. Seth Hastings Senior of Clinton New York, page 141-142. He graduated at the age of twenty-one from Rutgers College in the class of 1833. He studied law with Edwin M. Stanton, and removed to Angola, Indiana. He afterwards lived in Lima, Indiana, and in 1855 he moved to Council Bluffs, Iowa. He was elected Circuit Judge in 1867, and also held many other offices of trust, discharging every duty with honor and fidelity. The following is taken from a notice in the leading paper of the city at the time of his death; "Judge Douglass' life was one of more than ordinary activity and usefulness. And now that that life is ended, his great personal and professional worth and character, his high standing in the social circles of the community, and his established reputation as a successful lawyer and an upright jurist, furnish a fitting and sufficient epitaph. A man of excellent judgment and a shrewd observer of men, Judge Douglass combined with these qualifications unquestioned integrity and untiring industry." He was all life an earnest student of the best literature, and many curious and rare volumes were found in his library.
