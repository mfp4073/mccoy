  4. [S565] Source not known, Elzaphan Offill, son of John & Jane Offill, was born 4 September 1804 in Montgomery Co., Kentucky. He, as some of his brothers and sisters, went into Greenup Co., (later Carter Co.), Kentucky. There he met Martha Everman who was born 6 April 1810, daughter of John & Sarah Clark Everman.
    Martha's grandfather, William Everman, was born in England and met Margaret French in Virginia, married, and later came into the Bluegrass region of Kentucky, probably Harrodsburg. They were the parents of several children. Martha's father was John Everman who was born in 1777 in Potomac, Virginia. He married Sarah Clark, born 1787, Virginia from the same family as General George Rogers Clark.
    In 1808 John Everman brought his family as well as his father and settled on Barretts Creek about 5 miles west of Grayson, which was then in Greenup Co., Kentucky. He built his home of yellow poplar logs, hand-sawed and brick for the chimneys were burned on the farm.
    Martha's grandfather, William, died in Greenup Co., and was buried near John's home in what is known as Everman Graveyard. Her father, John Everman, was a farmer and a very prominent and influential pioneer. He was active in all the early affairs of Greenup Co.
