Cross of Montevideo, Minn., a stepson George Wall of Colfax, three grandchildren, 2 great grandchildren, 2 sisters, Mrs. Ivy Ward of Des Moines & Mrs. Eva Kaldenburg of Baraboo, Wis., a brother Clarence Main of Colfax. She was preceded in death by her daughter, Mrs. Lu Ella Fyrne McCallom, a son Robert Cross, her first husband Roy Cross & a granddaughter.

Delbert Cross

M, b. 31 May 1920, d. 3 December 1979
     His common name was Deb. Delbert Cross was born on 31 May 1920 at Colfax, Jasper Co., Iowa.1 He was the son of Roy E. Cross and Lu Ella Main. Delbert Cross married Louise Hayler on 26 July 1941 at Lineville, Missouri.1 Delbert Cross died on 3 December 1979 at Montevideo, Chippewa Co., Minnesota. Obituary.2 He was buried on 6 December 1979 at Montevideo, Chippewa Co., Minnesota.1

Citations

  1. [S27] Obituary of Delbert (Deb) Cross.
  2. [S27] Obituary of Delbert (Deb) Cross. Services Held for Delbert (Deb) Cross. Delbert (Deb) Cross, Northwestern National Life Insurance agent in Montevideo, Minnesota for many years died at his home in Montevideo on Monday at the age of 59 years. Death was caused by a heart condition. Funeral services were held Thursday, Dec. 6, at 2 p.m. at the Anderson Funeral Home Chapel with Rev. Richard Kennedy officiating. Hjalmer Quenemoen, Bennie Johnson, Al Toftness, Norman Chlestock, Bob Collins and Jessie Shaller were pall bearers and burial was in Montevideo. Delbert Cross was born May 31, 1920 at Colfax, Iowa, the son of Roy and Lu Ella Cross. He lived in Colfax for 23 years, Fergus Falls, 10 years, Minneapolis, three years and Montevideo 22 years. On July 26, 1941 he was united in marriage to Louise Hayler at Lineville, Missouri. To this union two daughters, Pamela and Deborah, were born. Having served in the U.S. Court Guard during WWII, Delbert became a member of the V.F.W. and was Chaplain for the Montevideo American Legion. He was also a member of the Masonic Lodge and was a member of the First Congregational Church of Montevideo. Immediate survivors include his wife Louise: one daughter, Deborah Cross of Portland, Oregon; two grandchildren, Dena and Lisa MacKenzie of Shakopee; one brother, Royal Cross of Colfax, and a son-in-law, Richard Coleman, Shakopee. Preceding him in death were a brother, Robert; sister, Fern; his parents and one daughter, Pamela Coleman, who died in October of this year. Arrangements were by Anderson Funeral Home.
