Lewis H. Alling

M, b. 26 April 1813, d. 26 March
     Lewis H. Alling was born on 26 April 1813 at Saratoga Co., New York.1 He married Sarah Ann Sibley, daughter of Derick Sibley and Abbey R. Risley, in 1841.2 Lewis H. Alling died on 26 March. Obituary.3

Citations

  1. [S213] Newspaper article from the Scrapbook/Album maintained by Caroline S. Hastings, page 40.
  2. [S212] Derick Sibley, Genealogy of the Sibley Family, page 37. They had two daughters.
  3. [S213] Newspaper article from the Scrapbook/Album maintained by Caroline S. Hastings, page 40. Lewis H. Alling. A Tribute to the Worth of the Deceased Pioneer. To the editor of the Morning Herald:
    One of the oldest of our business men, one of the most respected and best esteemed of our citizens, passed into the higher life at an early hour on the morning of March 26th. Lewis H. Alling was born in Saratoga county, New York, April 26, 1813. In his childhood he was a resident of Sodus, New York, and in 1833 came to Rochester, then about to assume municipal rank. His first vote was cast at the charter election when Jonathan Child was chosen mayor of the newly organized city. In business life he was identified with the production and manufacture of leather. For many years and until 1886, the firm name of Fitch & Alling and Alling Brothers was a landmark on Water street. Lewis H. Alling was always a devout man, without dissimulation, one who exemplified his Christian profession in word and work; of amiable temperament and benevolent disposition, a fair man, rendering alwasy a just and true account; talking cognizance rather of the good intent of men than of apparent errors; appreciating the good and tenderly covering the evil. As a matter of course, he was respected and liked. His countenance and help were sought by those who needed friends, and never sought in vain. There are now none to speak an ill word or to abate any tribute to his unsullied memory.
    He was in his youth a member of the Brick church, and in 1854 united with the organizers of the Central Presbyterian. Of this church he was a trustee and elder for eighteen or twenty years, always foremost among those who led in liberal financial contributions to the support of the church. Always devout, never austere or riged, a man of few prejudices, of simple faith, and believing in the effectuality of efforts to advance the moral standard and reform the erring, his name attached to any movement for such ends meant sincerity, earnestness, practical help in their promotion. The death of such a man is a loss felt in many circles and societies, in which good men associate, for beneficent work.
    Mr. Alling first married Sana Ann, daughter of Derick Sibley, a pioneer citizen who served several years in the state assembly, and was conspicuous for his ability and probity. By this marriage two daughters were born, Mrs. F.M. Ellery and Louise N. Alling, both surviving. After the death of Mrs. Alling in 1849, Mr. Alling married Abbey, daughter of the late Abner Wakelee, who survives her devoted and beloved husband. Messrs. S.Y. and William Alling are brothers still living here. Mrs. Farrand of Danville, Kentucky, and Mrs. H.A. Rew of Chicago are surviving sisters.
    The career of a good man, eminent for practical benevolence and preserving a record appropriate for emulation, is ever worth of extended notice. The writer believes he has not overstated the characteristics of Mr. Alling who has moved among us with equal and consistent step for more than a half century. F.S.R.
