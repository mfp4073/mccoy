Reuben Scheffers

M, d. December 1918
     Reuben Scheffers was the son of Govert Scheffers and Adriaana Messamaker. Reuben Scheffers died in December 1918. Obituary.1

Citations

  1. [S367] Letter from Mary Lou Marshall to James Benham dated 17 July 1997, page 8. The Spanish Influenza claimed as victims the past week two partners of the Prairie City Coal Company, only two days apart. Reuben Scheffers, the junior member of the partnership, was taken ill the first of the week and death came about 10:00 o'clock Sunday morning. He was a young man about thirty years of age and leaves a wife and three children besides a mother and several sisters. Chester Huckaby, the senior partner, passed away Tuesday afternoon about 4:00 p.m. He was a man of middle age, about forty-five, and also leaves a wife and three children to mourn his loss. Scheffers and Huckaby opened the Prairie City Coal Company mine on the Norris place about the first of last March, and all during the spring and summer months worked unceasingly, and it has been on a paying basis only for the last two or three months. The hard work and confinement in the mine had undermined their health and made them ready victims for the dreaded influenza. The two partners were brothers-in-law, Mr. Huckaby marrying a sister of Mr. Scheffer's. Funeral services and burial for Reuben Scheffers were held Sunday afternoon, and the services for Huckaby are to be held today.
