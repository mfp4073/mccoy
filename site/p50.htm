     Henry Marshall Gilbert was born on 25 December 1868 at Prairie City, Jasper Co., Iowa.1,2,3,4,5,6 He was the son of Samuel Turner Gilbert and Rebecca Jane Ball. Henry Marshall Gilbert married Lura Grace Vannoy, daughter of Cherrick John Noble and Violetta Evaline Angle, on 16 March 1907 at Grand Island, Hall Co., Nebraska.3 Henry Marshall Gilbert died on 10 January 1950 at Ogallala, Keith Co., Nebraska.3 He was buried at Ogallala Cemetery, Ogallala, Keith Co., Nebraska.3,7 Note.8

Amy Louisa Gilbert

F, b. 7 December 1870, d. 23 May 1932
     Amy Louisa Gilbert was born on 7 December 1870 at Jasper Co., Iowa.1,2,3,4 She was the daughter of Samuel Turner Gilbert and Rebecca Jane Ball. Amy Louisa Gilbert married George Evans on 13 March 1892 at Sutherland, Lincoln Co., Nebraska. Amy Louisa Gilbert died on 23 May 1932 at Sutherland, Lincoln Co., Nebraska.5,2,3 Obituary.6 She was buried on 25 May 1932 at Sutherland Riverview Cemetery, Sutherland, Lincoln Co., Nebraska.7

Amy Louisa Gilbert was born in Jasper County, Iowa, December 7, 1870 and passed away at her home in Sutherland, May 23, 1932 at the age of 61 years, 5 months and 16 days. As a girl of 15 years she moved with her parents to Keith County Nebraska, where they lived on a homestead. After graduation from the Ogallala schools she taught for several years in the county schools of Keith and Lincoln counties. March 13, 1892 she was united in marriage to George Evans. To this union were born 5 children, Birdie Mae who died at the home of her parents May 20, 1923. Samuel O. who died in France November 2, 1918, Margaret who died at the age of 3 years. Henry H who resides at La Junta, Colo. and Gladys F. who lives near Sutherland. Most of her married life has been spent in or near Sutherland with the exception of a short time in Keith and Phelps Counties. In the summer of 1930 she made the pilgrimage to France to the grave of her oldest son Samuel. She has been in failing health since the fall of 1930, but has been patient and uncomplaining through hours of suffering. As a young woman she joined the Presbyterian church and has been a faithful member ever since. She was a member of Sutherland Rebekah lodge and American Yeoman lodge whose secretary she has been for the past eight years. She has also been a devoted member of the Legion Auxiliary. She leaves her husband, two children, 13 grandchildren, three of them with their grandparents since the death of their mother in 1923. Two sisters and five brothers and a host of warm friends. Funeral services were held at the Presbyterian church Wednesday afternoon conducted by Rev. Knight of Hershey and internment was made in Riverview cemetery. Born December 7, 1870 in Jasper County, Iowa. She moved with parents at age of 15 (about 1885) from Iowa to Ogallala, Keith County, NB where they homesteaded.
