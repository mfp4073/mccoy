Mary Electa Wilson, daughter of Mary and Eli Wilson, was born June 4, 1842, in Farmington, Ill., and departed this life Aug. 11, 1924, at the home of her son in Ames, Ia. She was united in marriage to James Marion Gilbert on Feb. 14, 1868. To them were born two children, Eugene Pomeroy and Marion Dale, the latter of whom died in 1901. Immediately after their marriage this young couple moved to a farm near Prairie City, Ia., where they lived until 1887, when they came to Ames to give their sons the advantage of a college education. Mr. Gilbert died in 1913, and the subject of this sketch lived alone in their home until a few years ago, when the infirmities due to age caused her to leave her home and go to reside with her son. Mrs. Gilbert was for four years president of the W. R. C. of this city, and always much interested in its activities. She was also a member of the Ames Woman's club, and two organizations of the Methodist church--the Ladies' Aid society and the Woman's Home Missionary society. Of this latter organization she was a perpetual member. She leaves to mourn her passing one son and three grandchildren, Harold Marion, Neva Electa and Norma Elizabeth.

  20. [S52] Obituary of Sarah E. Gilbert. Mrs. Sarah E. Gilbert died at her residence one-half mile south of Prairie City on Monday morning after an illness lasting only a few days. Her death was quite unexpected up to a few hours previous to the time of its occurrence, although her health for some time was far from good. Sarah Elizabeth Gilbert was born in Carter Co., Kentucky, Dec 11, 1846[7]. She moved to this country with her parents in the spring of 1865. She united with the Christian church in 1865. In the spring of 1868, the 29th day of March she was united in marriage to Geo. W. Gilbert. To this marriage there were born eight children -- Malinda Jane, Mary Ellen, Pet, Jesse Morris, Lou Etta, James William, John Logan, George Raymond. They all survive her, except Pet who died Aug. 3, 1874. Mrs. Gilbert was aged 59 years, 4 months, and 19 days, her death occurring of April 30, 1906. Mrs. Gilbert was a woman of numberless kind deeds, who did all in her power for her children and friends. Her life was filled with hard work, and she always performed her duty as she saw it. Funeral services were held at the Christian church, Tuesday at 3 o'clock. The sermon was conducted by Elder C. H. Strawn, who paid a just tribute to a noble Christian woman! Her children were all present at the funeral services, as were also several grandchildren, and her venerable father, Mr. James Gilbert; her brothers, W. H. and Alden Gilbert, and her sister, Miss Amanda Gilbert. All her relatives and friends mourn her sincerely, realizing that a noble, self sacrificing mother has left them, but feel that she will enjoy everlasting rest and peace on a happier shore.

W. and Sarah Elwood was born February 27, 1856 in Jasper county, Iowa and died at the home of her son, Royce, on October 15, 1931 after a lingering illness at the age of 75 years 7 months and 18 days. She grew to young womanhood in Jasper county. On December 25, 1877 she was united in marriage with Franklin P. Gilbert. To this union six children were born, namely: Clyn E. Gilbert of Mallard, Iowa, Lucy A. Gilbert who died in infancy; Lulu J. Scheffers of Pella, Iowa, Florence J. Buck of Alpha, Minnesota and Royce E. Gilbert of Milford, Iowa. Most of her married life was spent in Guthrie county. They moved to Milford in 1920. Her husband preceded her in death on May 12, 1930. In her girlhood, she was united with the Line Star Presbyterian Church in Guthrie county and on July 23, 1909 she affiliated with the Eastern Star lodge at Plover, Iowa. She was a wonderful mother, her life just one long sacrifice for her children and others. She endured the hardships of early pioneer life. She leaves to mourn her death, two sisters, twenty-one grand-children and three great grandchildren who will cherish her memory forever. Funeral services were held in the Methodist church last Saturday afternoon conducted by the pastor, Rev. W. S. Richards and the Eastern Star Lodge of which she was a member.
