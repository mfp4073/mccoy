Govert Fred Scheffers, son of Adriana and Govert Scheffers was born in Pella May 8th, 1883 and passed away Tuesday evening, September 18th, 1945 at the age of 62 years, 4 months and 10 days. He grew to young manhood and has spent most of his life in this vicinity. He was united in marriage to Elmay Gilbert November 10th, 1909 at Audubon, Iowa. To this union two children were born, Mrs. Doris Dingeman and Elbert Scheffers of Pella. Mr. Scheffers was a carpenter by trade but due to ill health has done shop work here in town the last few years. He has been a member of the Pella Gun Club for years and truly enjoyed sports. He was a kind and devoted husband and father and a loyal friend. He leaves to mourn his death, his wife, two children, four sisters, Mrs. Nettie Curry of Spencer, Mrs. Nellie Marshall and Mrs. Dena Warner of Prairie City and Mrs. Minnie Jenkins of Adel, Iowa and a host of other relatives and friends who will cherish his memory forever. A cherished one from us has gone, A voice we loved is still, With humble hearts we bow our heads, And seek to do God's will. Sad is the home a loved one is taken. Vacant his chair, empty his bed. God called, and Fred answered his summons. The spirit from his temple to Jesus has fled. Weep not dear cousins for this one departed, He's happy up there with his loved ones we know. Just lean upon Jesus in this hour of sorrow. Upon you, dear ones, his loved he'll bestow. Written by Josephine Wise. In memory of Fred to the Scheffers Family. CARD OF THANKS We wish to acknowledge publicly our indebtedness to friends and neighbors for many kind and thoughtful acts performed for us and our dear husband and father, Govert Fred Scheffers. We are grateful to all who expressed sympathy and to those who sent flowers. Mrs. Fred Scheffers and Children.

  14. [S76] Obituary of Frederick Jacob Buck. Death, following several months of patient suffering came to Fred Jacob Buck, prominent Wisconsin township farmer at the Buck home Friday September 17, Mr. Buck's affliction being an incurable disease. Mr. Buck was a gentleman possessed of a quiet but pleasing manner, and because of his sterling honesty had won a large circle of friends among his neighbors in Wisconsin township, where he was always ready to take part in any civic or church activity that had for its purpose the building of a better community. It is certain that his absence in the Wisconsin community will not only be missed by his immediate family, but also by his large circle of friends. Fred Jacob Buck was born June 14, 1885, at Union Hill, Illinois, where he grew to manhood. On October 26, 1910, Mr. Buck was united in marriage to Miss Florence Irene Gilbert, and for the past 28 years the Bucks have resided on their farm southwest of Alpha. The union of Mr. and Mrs. Buck has been blessed by the birth of seven children. Surviving are his wife, and the following children: Viola, (Mrs. Dean Rowe) of Jackson; Gilbert of Alpha; Ruby (Mrs. Wesley Edwards) of Jackson; Leonard of Alpha; Arnold of Jackson, and Virgle and Irene of Alpha; also six grandchildren. During the long residence in the Alpha community, Mr. Buck had won the friendship of a large number of admiring friends. He was a member of the Alpha Presbyterian church, having been confirmed in early life in the Lutheran faith. Funeral services were held on Monday afternoon at two o'clock from the Buck home in Wisconsin township and at 2:30 o'clock from the Presbyterian church in Jackson, the services being conducted by the Revs. James Young of Alpha and Paul B. Shedd of Jackson. There was a profusion of beautiful flowers as the remains were laid to rest in Riverside cemetery.
