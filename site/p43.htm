George Washington Gilbert was born in Carter Co. Kentucky, November 2, 1845. He came to this country with his parents in the spring of 1865 where he spent the remainder of his life. In the spring of 1868, the 29th day of March, he was united in marriage of Sarah E. Gilbert. To this marriage there were born eight children: Malinda Jane, Mary Ellen, Pet, Jesse Morris, Lou Etta, James William, John Logan, and George Raymond. They all survive him except Pet, who died August 3, 1874. Mr. Gilbert believed and took part in the further development of the country in which he lived. He was a friend of the poor and did many kind deeds in their behalf. He leaves to mourn his loss seven children, sixteen grand children, two brothers and two sisters, Robert Gilbert, Frank Gilbert, Amy Weaver and Lucinda Weaver. The funeral service was held at the Christian Church on Monday at 2:00 o'clock and was attended by a large number of friends and relatives. Rev C. H. Strawn, of Altoona, a former pastor here and a friend of the deceased, preached the sermon. Interment in the Westview Cemetery. His children were all present, also his brother and wife, Mr. and Mrs. Frank Gilbert, of Coon Rapids, Limon [sic] Weaver, of Newton, Pomeroy Gilbert, of Ames, and Hiram Wiggins and wife, of Des Moines. His relatives and many friends mourn his death very much but feel that he has gone to another shore where sorrow and grief are never known and where he will enjoy everlasting peace. CARD OF THANKS: We wish to convey out most sincere thanks and appreciation to our dear friends and neighbors who so kindly remembered us with their kind acts and beautiful flowers during our bereavement. Brothers, sister, and children: Mr. and Mrs. Alfred Barrett, Mr. and Mrs. Frank Anderson, Mr. and Mrs. Lee Taylor, Mr. and Mrs. John Gilbert, Mr. and Mrs. Ray Gilbert, Jesse Gilbert, and James Gilbert.

  20. [S322] Newspaper article concerning George W. Gilbert. COURT HANDS DOWN DECISION: Ruling is Made that Deeds of Conveyance by George W. Gilbert are Sustained. Judge H. F. Wagner handed down a decision in the case of Gilbert vs. Ruggles Tuesday afternoon, holding that the deeds were valid. This was an action brought by the heirs of Geo. W. Gilbert, deceased, to set aside two deeds of conveyance executed by Mr. Gilbert, one for one hundred acres of land to J. R. Ruggles and Amanda Ruggles, his wife, in 1910 and the other for one hundred acres deeded by him to five minor children of J. R. and Amanda Ruggles, dated April 11, 1914. Both deeds were sought to be set aside on the ground that Mr. Gilbert was of unsound mind at the time they were made and that they were secured through fraud and undue influence. The case was hotly contested on both sides, the trial occupying the attention of the court for over a week. Mulvaney & Mulvaney of Des Moines, and E. M. S. McLaughlin of this city were attorneys for the Gilbert heirs, and McCoy & McCoy of Oskaloosa, and E. J. Salmon of this city represented the Ruggles. Judge Wagner handed down his opinion this afternoon sustaining the validity of both deeds, holding that the Gilbert heirs had failed to establish a case against either of the conveyances upon any of the grounds upon which they claimed the deeds should be set aside, and taxed the costs of suit against the heirs, who say that they intend to appeal the case to the supreme court.

    A story regarding John & Sarah Tabor Gilbert's early days in KY.: The story was written by Anna Roe Kilgallin the great granddaughter of John & Sarah (Tabor) Gilbert, regarding John and Sarah's move to Ky., after 1815 from Tazewell Co., VA., when they moved into a cabin in KY.
    "The first night it caught fire and burned forcing them to live in a cave for the duration of the Summer, until a new cabin could be built. The couple managed to rescue their belongings. They hung quilts over the opening of the cave. In their garden plot they had planted sweet potatoes and other vegetables. One night their little dog spent the night barking as it kept behind the quilts. The next morning the garden was full of bear tracks. This article was found, by Larry Gray, in "Shakin & Diggin" printed Dec 1983 page 11. A book published in Vanceburg, KY. 41179. The Historical Society in that region.

  13. [S140] Obituary of James Madison Gilbert. James Madison Gilbert, born in Russell County, Virginia, Nov. 13, 1817, died at his home near Prairie City, Iowa, Dec. 18, 1908, aged 91 years, 1 month and 5 days. At the age of 10 years he moved with his parents to Carter County, Kentucky, where he lived until the spring of 1865, when he moved to Iowa, one and one-half miles north of Prairie City, where he had lived ever since -- almost 45 years -- until his death. He was married twice, his first wife being Charlotte Wilburn, to which union was born one child, Sarah E. Gilbert who was born Dec 11, 1847. November 11, 1852 he was married to his second wife, Sarah J. Offill, who died March 28, 1899. To them were born twelve children, seven of who preceded them to the spirit land, and five survive them -- John B. of Audubon, Iowa, Martha J. Durbin of Dedham, Iowa, W. H. Gilbert, Amanda Gilbert and Alden Gilbert of Prairie City, Iowa, all of whom are present here today. He also leaves one brother Samuel Gilbert of Grand Island, Nebr. He has twenty-one grand-children and seventeen great grand-children. He united with the Christian church and had been a member for over seventy years. He was a kind husband and father and a good neighbor. He had kind words for all in sorrow or in need. He was respectfully and affectionately called in his later life, Uncle Jimmie. CARD OF THANKS: We wish to extend our heartfelt thanks to the friends and neighbors for their assistance and sympathy during the sickness and death of our beloved father. We especially wish to thank those business men and others who contributed many beautiful wreaths of flowers. Children of James Gilbert.

Amy J. Gilbert

F, b. 5 March 1824, d. 13 March 1903
     Amy J. Gilbert was born on 5 March 1824 at Tazewell Co., Virginia.1,2,3,4,5,6,7 She was the daughter of John Gilbert and Sarah Tabor.8 Amy J. Gilbert married Robert Jasper Henderson, son of James Alexander Henderson and Jane Miller, on 31 May 1838.1,3,4,5,8,9,7 Amy J. Gilbert died on 13 March 1903 at Carter Co., Kentucky.1,3,10,5,7
