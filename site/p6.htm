  5. [S493] Obituary from Find a Grave Website, Dr. Zephaniah Hull was born in Wallingford, now Cheshire, Conn. He and his family moved to Bethlehem, Conn., in about 1754, joining the church that year from the New Cheshire Parish. Dr. Hull was "a great friend of the celebrated Dr. Bellamy, and recognized as a man of high character and great influence both as physician and citizen."[The Doolittle Family In America] He and his wife died the same day, November 10, 1760, of the "great sickness" (a malignant pleurisy.) Two of their children died within a week. Soon after these deaths, "one Deacon Strong coming by, raised a flock of eleven quails, which flew over the. . .house, and dropped in the garden; immediately after three rose and flew into the bushes, but the other eight were picked up dead."[John W. Barber, History and Antiquities of Every Town in Connecticut] This was thought to be symbolic of the fact that only three in the family survived. This story was told by their daughter Lydia. In all, 34 people died of the "great sickness" in November of that year in the small town of Bethlehem.
