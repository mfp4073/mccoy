Herscher, 69, died Friday at the Davidson hospital in Lake City, after undergoing an operation. Funeral services were Monday at the Methodist church, Havelock, in charge of Rev. W. F. Schneck of the Rolfe Presbyterian church, assisted by Rev. J. D. Wolcott. A quartet composed of Mrs. Frank Kirby, Mrs Oertley, Carl Barth and Olin Young sang "Does Jesus Care," "My Father Knows," and "Have Thine Own Way, Lord. The active pall bearers were nephews of the deceased: LeRoy Miller, Elva Buck, LeRoy Miller, Melvin Buck, Gilbert Buck, and Eldon Buck. Honorary pall bearers were G. A. Gruver, Fred Hauswirth, Charles Mann, James Wright, Clarence Cornwell, Shelby Glenn. Michael George Herscher was born on January 18, 1872, in Kankakee County, Illinois, and died Friday, October 31, at Lake City, Iowa, being 69 years, 9 months and 13 days of age. At the age of 14 years he was confirmed in the faith of the Lutheran church and stayed by that faith until his death. In 1896 he was united in marriage to Martha Buck of Union Hill. To this union three children were born: Mrs. Lloyd Emory, Mrs. Earl Sandy and Harry Herscher all of Havelock. In 1903 the family moved to a farm south of Havelock where they lived until the spring of 1924, when he and Mrs. Herscher moved to a residence in Rolfe, Iowa, where he was engaged in the implement business for three years after which he returned to Havelock, where he resided until his death. He spent his later years in writing insurance. He leaves to mourn his loss, his wife, three children, his mother, 93 years of age, one sister, Mrs. Lena Cross of Kankakee, Illinois, four brothers, William of Ovid, Michigan, Albert of Owasso, Michigan, Henry of Warsaw, Indiana, and Benjamin of Pierceton, Indiana, nine grandchildren and one great grandchild. He had three serious operations in 1930 and 1931 and has been a very patient and silent sufferer since. He submitted to another operation last Thursday from which he did not survive. He will be greatly missed.

  3. [S77] Obituary of William Henry Buck. Last Rites for William H. Buck Held, Sunday. Died of Injuries Resulting From Fall from Truck. Relatives and friends gathered at the McIntire Funeral home, Sunday, Sept. 29, at 2:30 p.m. to pay their last respects to William Buck, 61, who died Friday in the Lutheran hospital in Fort Dodge of injuries received when he fill from a truck, Thursday. Funeral services were in charge of the Rev. W.F. Schneck, pastor of the Presbyterian church. Mrs. Grant Johnson and Mrs. Art Mickalek, accompanied by Mrs. Fred Nelson, sang "Sometime We'll Understand," "Beautiful Isle of Somewhere" and "He Knows." Mr. and Mrs. W. L. McIntire took charge of the flowers and pall bearers, six nephews of the deceased, were Leroy Miller and Alva Buck of Rolfe, Melvin Buck and Harry Herscher of Havelock and Gilbert and Leonard Buck of Alpha, Minn. Burial was in Powhatan cemetery near Plover. The following obituary, with the exception of the time of his death, was written by the deceased in the year 1928. William Henry Buck was born in Bergedorf, Germany on the 29th of June, 1879 and died on September 27, 1940, at the age of 61 years, 2 months, and 27 days. He was confirmed in the German language in the German Lutheran church near Bonfield, Ill., on the 7th day of April, 1895. He was the second oldest son of William and Wilhelmine Buck, the oldest son having died in infancy. William's father died on December 18, 1919 and his mother on the 9th of June, 1927. When William was three years old, he moved with his parents from Germany to near Bonfield, Ill., where he went to the common public schools. William's parents lived in Illinois for about 20 years and then in the spring of 1902 they moved from Illinois to near Rolfe, Iowa, on the farm. They lived here until 1910 when they retired and moved to Havelock, Iowa, on account of William's father's health. William was never married and so made his home mostly with his father and mother up to the time of their death. After his parents' death he made his home with his sister, Mrs. George Miller of Rolfe, for 13 years. He leaves to mourn his departure, four brothers and two sisters as follows: Sarah, who is Mrs. George Miller, Richard and Louis of Rolfe, Iowa; Martha, who is Mrs. Mike Herscher and Henry of Havelock, and Fred of Alpha, Minn., besides many nieces and nephews. He will be sadly missed by the remaining brothers and sisters and by the other relatives and friends as well.

It was in this rural setting that he was confirmed in the Lutheran faith. When he was 21, his family moved to Iowa and located on a farm three miles south of Plover. He was married to Maggie Miller in 1905 and they settled on a farm south of Plover where they lived until 1942 when they retired and moved to Rolfe. To this union 10 children were born. He is survived by his wife, Maggie; two sons, Dale of Rolfe and Alva of Barnum; and four daughters, Mrs. Harold (Helen) O'Neall of Plover, Mrs. Aulden (Alma) Oxley of Decorah, Mrs. Louis (Lu Ella) Christensen of Mt.
