Adriana Messamaker, daughter of Tonie and William Messamaker, was born in Holland Jan. 5, 1850, and departed this life Jan. 24, 1937, at the age of 87 years and 19 days. When a small child she came to this country with her parents, and on the 18th day of August in 1868 at Pella, Iowa, she was united in marriage to Govert Scheffers, who preceded her in death in 1906 at the age of 66 years. Mrs. Scheffers was the mother of nine children: the first child Johnny, died at the age of five, and Reuben passed away in 1918 at the age of thirty-one. Those who remain to mourn her death are Mrs. Nellie Marshall, Mrs. Dena Warner and John Scheffers of Prairie City, Fred of Pella, William of Coon Rapids, Iowa, and Mrs. Minnie Jenkins of Waukee, Iowa, also Mrs. Netty Curry of Spencer, Iowa: twenty-six grandchildren and a number of great-grandchildren. She leaves three sisters, Mrs. Wm. Wright, Mrs. James Archer, and Mrs. Thomas Welch, all of Des Moines, and one brother Cornelius Messamaker of Harvey, Iowa, besides many other relatives and friends. In her young womanhood Mrs. Scheffers united with the Methodist church at Mitchellville, transferring her membership from there to the Methodist church in Pella and later to the church in Prairie City, where she was a faithful member until her death. Although she was prevented from attending the services during the last years of her life, she always maintained an interest in the church and its welfare. She was a kind and devoted wife and mother, loyal to her friends, of whom there were many, and generously helpful at all times to her neighbors. The funeral services were conducted from the Walters' funeral home Tuesday, Jan. 26, at 1:30 p.m. Rev. J. H. Findley officiating. Mrs. Ethel Williams, Miss Nettie De Gooyer, Louis Vander Linden and H.M. Bowans sang "Tell It To Jesus" and "God Will Take Care Of You," Miss Dessa Blodgett at the piano. Burial was made in Graceland cemetery at Pella, Iowa. Pallbearers were Frank Shearer, Carl Wiggins, Everett Perryman, C. W. Phelps, Roy Wiggins and Chas. Phearman.
