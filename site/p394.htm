Howard VanAtta1

M
     Howard VanAtta is the son of Ray Hull VanAtta.1 Howard VanAtta married Grace Eleanor Groff, daughter of John David Groff and Lydia Hunt, at Grace Methodist Protestant Church.2

Citations

  1. [S522] Wedding Announcement of Grace Groff. The Union Register,.
  2. [S522] Wedding Announcement of Grace Groff. The Union Register, Grace Groff Chooses White Satin for Wedding Saturday. Wearing an ivory satin directoire gown with a long court train, trimmed with family rose-paint lace, and carrying an arm bouquet of gardenias and Johanna Hill roses, Miss Grace Groff, daughter of Mr. and Mrs. John David Groff, of Columbus, became the bride of Howard VanAtta, son of Mr. and Mrs. Ray Hull VanAtta, of Rye Beach. The ceremony was performed Saturday afternoon, in the Grace Methodist Protestant church, with Rev. Frank Lawrence Brown officiating. While the guests were assembling, Mrs. Frank Brown played a program of music and Mr. Robert Cockerell sang. The bride entered the church with her father. Mrs. Harold Nelson of Cincinnati, was matron of honor and Miss Mary Marguerite Gaal, 175 Deshler avenue, Columbus, was maid of honor. Their gowns were in autumn shades of green and rust taffeta fashioned along directoire lines. The full shirts fell in slight trains and they wore velvet bonnets in colors matching their gowns and carried bronze anemones and chrysanthemums. Mr. John A. Rousculp, brother-in-law of the bride, was best man. The ushers were Mr. Lawrence E. Taber, Mr. Walter L. Virtue, Mr. Cassius H. Makeever, and Mr. Frederick W. Wieland, all of Mt. Gilead. For her daughter's wedding Mrs. Groff chose a dress of royal blue velvet and wore a corsage of roses. Mrs. Rousculp, sister of the bride, wore a wine colored velvet dress and a corsage of yellow roses, while Mrs. VanAtta, mother of the bridegroom, was dressed in a brown crepe dress and wore a corsage of roses also. Following the ceremony a reception was held at the bride's home. After the middle of October, the couple will reside at 135 West High street, Mt. Gilead. The bride was graduated from Ohio State university and is affiliated with Alpha Sigma Alpha sorority. She has been teaching in the Alliance high school but was formerly a teacher of Home Economics in Mt. Gilead high school. Mr. VanAtta attended Ohio State university and is affiliated with the State Highway department at the Delaware office.
