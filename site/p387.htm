She asked us if we were going to graduate, and we laughed at her. She got so mad to the point she yelled at us. We stopped and looked at her and she said, “you are going to graduate if you like it or not!" From then on she pushed our limits. None of us knew what we were capable of. She pushed me the hardest because she saw my past on my body.

    A lot of the crimes that happen are people in foster care, group homes or on the streets. That's the only way we know how to survive. Animals and kids that have been abused are different but on the same road.

A lot of people in the world don't see it as it really is. They deny it. Well, in my family a lot of abuse happens, and neglect, but people ignore it as it never happened. I try to do that every day, but for my career I have to face it. Give me hope or at least a father figure; I'm not the only one. Sorry for asking so much of you, but you are the last hope we have as kids in the system. Could you meet us one day or at least meet with kids in the system that are trying to survive?

  4. [S511] Obituary of Elizabeth Markley Teel. Mrs. Teel Dead. Highly Respected Lady Sleeps the Last Long Sleep. Mrs. Elizabeth Markley Teel died at her home east of Sulphur Spring, Saturday morning, at 7 o'clock, aged 75 years. She was a widow of George W. Teel, who was well known in Bucyrus and Crawford county. Mrs. Teel has suffered for months and her death was not unexpected but is none the less regretted by her many friends. Elizabeth Markley was born in Liberty township seventy-five years ago. She was one of the most earnest Christian ladies of the neighborhood and was a fond and faithful wife and mother. She resided in the vicinity of her birth nearly her entire life and was one of the best known and most highly respected and esteemed ladies in the neighborhood. September 17, 1844 she was married to George W. Teel, at that time a farmer but who later was employed in various constructive work. He was especially valuable in the work of promoting the road which was afterward known as the T. & O. C. In 1878 the family removed to Bucyrus where Mr. Teel was assignee of a large estate. The next year the family removed to Crestline and a year later returned to the farm east of Sulphur Springs. There were seven children surviving, she also leaving two brothers. The brothers are Elias Markley, living near Nevada, and John Markley, of Wood county. The children are Mrs. Laura Ebert, of Elyria; Mrs. Grant Zerbe, of Sandusky City; George and Marcus Teel and Mrs. Eila Michaelis, of Washington state; Leander L. Teel who resides somewhere in the west and Jared Teel who is at home. There are a number of grandchildren and great-grand children. Mrs. Teel was a life-long member of the Lutheran church and was a sincere and earnest Christian. Her character was a pure and beautiful one and her influence was always for good and for the help and uplifting of those about her. She was a kind and devoted mother, a good neighbor and a valuable and esteemed member of the community where she made her home. Her friends will deeply regret her death and she will be sadly missed by the many who knew her and loved her.
