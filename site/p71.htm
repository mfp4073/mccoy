  2. [S25] Obituary of Louis E. Main. Funeral services were held Tuesday at the Colfax Methodist Church for Louis Edward Main, 70, former Prairie City area resident. He died March 18 at the home of a daughter in Collins. The Rev. Dale Nicholson, minister of the church, conducted the services. Burial was at Waveland Cemetery in Prairie City. Military grave side rites were conducted by Jones-Willis Post of American Legion of Colfax. Mr. Main is survived by a daughter, Mrs. Forest Maxwell of Collins; a son, Orville William Main of Tinley Park, Ill.; three sisters, Mrs. Cleo Wall of Colfax; Mrs. Eva Kaldenberg of North Freedom, Wis., and Mrs. William R. Ward of Des Moines; a brother, Clarence of Colfax; six grandchildren. A veteran of World War I, Mr. Main was a member of the American Legion. The son of Mr. and Mrs. Lewis Main, he was born Feb. 28, 1892, in Clarks, Nebr. He moved to Iowa with his parents at the age of two, residing in the Prairie City and Colfax communities until three years ago when he moved to the home of his daughter at Collins. He was married to Beulah McCarty Nov. 14, 1923 at Oskaloosa. He was a road and maintenance man in Jasper County until his retirement.
