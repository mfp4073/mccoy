  3. [S213] Newspaper article from the Scrapbook/Album maintained by Caroline S. Hastings, page 8. Death of Edwin O. Hall. Died: EDWIN O. HALL, In Falmouth, Maine, Wednesday, Sept. 19th, 1883, Edwin O. Hall, of Honolulu, Sandwich Islands, in the 73d year of his age. His remains will be taken to Honolulu for burial.
    To the Editor of the Democrat and Chronicle: Sir: Mr. Hall was a nephew of the late Derrick and Levi Sibley; also of Mrs. Edwin Serantom, late of this city. He went to the Sandwich Islands in 1834 as printer to the board of missionaries. While so employed he was sent to Oregon, and set up the first printing press ever run west of the Rocky mountains. Returning to Honolulu, he published the official paper of the government. After several years he resigned this position and engaged in mercantile pursuits; establishing one of the leading commercial houses in Honolulu, that of E. O. Hall & Son. He was at one time secretary of the interior, and afterwards a member of the king's privy council.
         Mr. Hall was formerly a resident of Rochester and will be remembered by our older citizens. He was twice married; his first wife accompanying him on their wedding journey to the Sandwich Islands. She died in 1876, leaving two surviving children, a son and daughter who reside in Honolulu. Mr. Hall was visiting friends in this country, and was taken with the fatal illness while at the residence of the father of his present wife.
         He was a man of singular purity of soul; exemplary in his life, fearless in his death. While he will be missed in the community so long adored, his name will be consecrated with the virtues that make "the memory of the just smell sweet, and blossom in the dust." Rochester, N. Y., Sept. 27, 1883.
