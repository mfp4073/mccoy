Tyler Ralphs Hunt1

M, b. 8 March 1946
     Tyler Ralphs Hunt was born on 8 March 1946.2 He is the son of Theodore Perry Hunt and Elizabeth Wilma Pugh.1 Tyler Ralphs Hunt married Denna Lee Sneltjes, daughter of William Lawrence Sneltjes, on 24 August 1967 at Piedmont Community Church, Piedmont, Alameda Co., California.3

Citations

  1. [S539] Wedding Announcement for Tyler Hunt. Oakland Tribune,.
  2. [S554] Dakota R. Baldry, Colonists and Their Descendants, page 38.
  3. [S539] Wedding Announcement for Tyler Hunt. Oakland Tribune, page 33, Justwed Hunts Off to Hawaii. Piedmont Community Church was the setting for the afternoon wedding uniting Deena Lee Sneltjes and Tyler Ralphs Hunt. After the ceremony the couple received guests at the Women's Athletic Club and left for a honeymoon trip to the Hawaiian Islands. The bride, daughter of Mr. and Mrs. William Lawrence Sneltjes of Oakland, wore an English net cage with chapel train appliqued with Venise lace over a dress of white peau de soie. A long illusion veil fell from a bonnet of matching Venise lace, especially designed for the bride, and she carried a bouquet of white roses. The bride's attendants wore yellow chiffon gowns, styled along Empire lines, and carried white daisies. Maid of honor was Gretchen Gingg and bridesmaids were Mary Lou Kilburn, Kathy Griffith, Sherry Wohlrab and Marty Leaver. Carrie and Sherri Hunt, nieces of the bridegroom were flower girls. They were dressed in white organza over pale yellow dresses with moss green velvet sashes. Steven Hunt was best man for his brother. They are the sons of Mr. and Mrs. Theodore Hunt. Ushering duties were taken by Michael Porter, Gary Lion, Charles De Lauer III and Richard Ludlow. Deena took her studies at Anna Head School and at Colorado Women's College. The young man attended Fullerton State College.
