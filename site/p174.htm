Beulah Amelia Sanders

F, b. 9 March 1887, d. 15 September 1959
     Beulah Amelia Sanders was born on 9 March 1887 at Carlisle, Warren Co., Iowa.1 She was the daughter of David Franklin Sanders and Mary Elizabeth Owens. Beulah Amelia Sanders married Carl Kail, son of Will D. Kail and Cora Hull, on 16 June 1909 at Carlisle, Warren Co., Iowa.1 Beulah Amelia Sanders died on 15 September 1959 at Covina, Los Angeles Co., California.1 Obituary.2 She was buried at Carlisle Cemetery, Carlisle, Warren Co., Iowa.

Children of Beulah Amelia Sanders and Carl Kail

  • Phillip Bernard Kail+ b. 16 Jul 1910
  • Wilton Berkley Kail+ b. 15 Sep 1912, d. 17 Oct 1974
  • Richard Carl Kail+ b. 29 Jul 1926

Citations

  1. [S178] Shirley Shaffer Lettington and Michelle Lettington Bobro, A Royal Heritage, page 227.
  2. [S176] Obituary of Beulah Sanders Kail. Beulah Sanders Kail. Beulah Sanders Kail, daughter of Mr. and Mrs. David Sanders, was born March 9, 1887 in Carlisle, Iowa. She attended the Carlisle schools and following graduation became a teacher. On June 16, 1909 she was united in marriage to Dr. Carl Kail, eldest son of Mr. and Mrs. Will Kail, also of Carlisle. They made their home in Stratford, Iowa where Dr. Kail practiced medicine for several years. He entered military service in World War I as a doctor with the troops and for a time the family resided on the east coast. At the close of the war, the Kails settled in Lincoln, Nebraska where the doctor was an x-ray specialist. Since the death of her husband in 1950, Mrs. Kail has maintained her own home here in Carlisle.
    She was a member of Rebekah Lodge and the American Legion Auxiliary. In girlhood she united with the Carlisle Baptist Church and has always been actively interested in the work. "She served the Lord with gladness," having served as deaconess and was treasurer of the Missionary Circle for the past several years, which office she was holding at the time of her death September 15, 1959 in a Covina, California hospital. She had gone west a few weeks ago to visit her sons.
    She was preceded in death by her parents, her husband, one brother, Garland Sanders, one sister, Mrs. Marion Wright, wife of the late H. J. Wright. She is survived by three sons, Phillip and Richard Kail of Denver, Colorado and Wilton of Covina, California; two sisters, Mrs. Bessie Gilbert, wife of Dr. Gilbert of Chandler, Arizona, and Mrs. Helen Schooler, wife of G. D. Schooler of Carlisle; three granddaughter; two grandsons, and a host of relatives and friends.
