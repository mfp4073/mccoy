He fought in World War I with the 107th Infantry of the New York National Guard, obtaining the rank of Major in the U.S. Army in overseas service. During the periods between and following World War I and World War II he commanded the local National Guard and Home Guard units of the 105th Infantry of the New York National Guard, with the rank of Major. His political offices held during this period were that of Postmaster and Town Clerk in Malone, New York. In addition to being a devoted father and sportsman, he is active in Community Civic and Veterans affairs, holding important positions in both fields.
