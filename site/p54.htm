Winegar, 84, a lifelong resident of the Prairie City area, were held at 1:30 p.m. Saturday, March 15, 1980 at Walters-Coburn Funeral Home in Prairie City. He died Thursday, March 13 at Iowa Lutheran Hospital in Des Moines. The Rev. Alexander Haines, pastor of the Prairie City Christian Church, conducted services. Special music was provided by organist Mrs. Richard Smith and soloist William Van Zee; special hymns were "Beyond the Sunset" and "How Great Thou Art." Casket bearers were Dennis Winegar, Kenneth Winegar, Robert Winegar, Larry Winegar, Dean Kletting and Don Stevenson. Grave side rites were conducted by Prairie City Booth-De Vries American Legion at Waveland Cemetery in Prairie City. Taps were played by Robert Boyston. Survivors are his wife, Erma Lee; and two sisters Mrs. Thomas (Josephine) Briles and Mrs. Carl (Grace) Kletting, both of Prairie City. He was preceded in death by his parents, three brothers and two sisters. A World War I veteran, Mr. Winegar was a member of the American Legion and was a longtime member of the Prairie City Band. He had been a farmer, a city employee for many years, and was employed at the Dowden Company in Prairie City. The son of David H. and Mary Ellen Breckenridge Winegar, he was born November 24, 1895 in Prairie City. He was united in marriage to Erma Lee Barrett April 28, 1934 in Mitchellville.
