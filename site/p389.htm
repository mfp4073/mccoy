  3. [S520] Obituary of John A. Sheetz. Cleveland Pain Dealer, John A. Sheetz, 63, Banker, Dies. John A. Sheetz, trust officer of the Cleveland Trust Co., died of cancer yesterday in St. Luke's Hospital. He joined the corporate trust department of the Cleveland Trust Co. in 1931. He was made assistant trust officer in 1935. Since January, 1951, he had been trust officer. Mr. Sheetz's banking career in the Cleveland area began in 1921, when he was appointed cashier and general manager of the former First National Bank of Willoughby. It was following the liquidation of that bank in 1931 that he joined Cleveland Trust. World War I Vet. Born 63 years ago in New Washington, O., he was a graduate of Capital University, Columbus, in 1913. Until 1917 he worked as cashier of the New Washington Bank. His grandfather and father both were bankers in that city. Mr. Sheetz enlisted in 1917 in the army ordnance department; later he was commissioned in the quartermaster corps. Following his military service he entered the graduate school of business of Harvard University, finishing there in 1920 with a master degree. Mr. Sheetz was a charter member of the Willoughby Kiwanis and was a past president of the Willoughby Chamber of Commerce. He was president of the Willoughby Credit Men's Association. He was active in Palmer Roberts Post, American Legion. From 1932 to 1934 Mr. Sheetz was president of the Cleveland Safe Deposit Association; also he had served on several committees of the National Safe Deposit Advisory Council. He was a past member of the University Club, a member of the Harvard Club of Cleveland, and had served as president of the Harvard Business School Club of Cleveland. Amateur Gardener. On the spacious grounds of his home, Midland Moor, Mentor, he was able to indulge his skill as an amateur gardener. Surviving him are his wife, the former Hazel Yaeger of Litchfield, Ill., to whom he was married in 1921. There also are a daughter, Mrs. Lucille Exton; a son, John I. Sheetz and two grandchildren. Memorial services will be at 1:30 p.m. Saturday in the home of the Unitarian Society, Euclid Avenue at E. 82d Street. In place of flowers the family has suggested that contributions be made to the Memorial Building Fund of First Unitarian Church, Shaker Heights, or to the American Cancer Society, 233 Arcade.

  10. [S528] Wedding Announcement of Laura Hunt. Laura Hunt Marries Edward Strong In Rites at Piedmont Church. Before an arrangement of chrysanthemums, larkspur and magnolia leaves on the alter of the Piedmont Community Church last Saturday night, Miss Laura Hunt became the bride of Mr. Edward Franklin Strong. C. Perry Hunt escorted his daughter down the taper-lighted aisle and the wedding lines were read by Dr. Holland F. Burr. A princess-styled rose blush pearl satin gown, fashioned with a sweetheart neckline, was worn by the bride. Her Mary Queen of Scots headpiece of heirloom rosepoint lace, originally worn by the groom's maternal great-great-grandmother, held in place her finger tip veil of tulle which was combined with rare lace from her mother's wedding veil. She carried white cattleya orchids and lilies-of-the-valley. In the bridal procession, Mrs. Karl F. Dittman, the bride's sister, as honor matron, Miss Dorothy Williams as honor maid and Mrs. Ted P. Hunt, Laura's sister-in-law, wore identical frocks of gold tapestry brocade and their headdress and bouquets were of chrysanthemums which shaded from gold to bronze. Pearl chokers, gifts of the bride, were worn by each. Addison Strong, J., was best man for his brother, while Ward Madiera, Russell Pearce, George Parrish and Sam Shannon seated the guests. The justweds received the well wishes of their family and friends at the Claremont Country Club. Mrs. Hunt greeted the wedding guests in a full length Chantilly lace gown of sapphire blue. Her hat was of blue maline and velvet ribbon. White orchids completed her ensemble. The groom's mother, Mrs. Addison G. Strong, wore a cyclamen toned creation of Chantilly lace and her matching maline hat was trimmed with tiny ostrich tips. Her corsage was of gold orchids. Mr. and Mrs. Strong are now honeymooning at the San Ysidro Inn in the Ojai Valley. They will make their home in Berkeley. The outstretched hands of Dorothy Williams caught the bridal bouquet, as the new Mrs. Strong, wearing a beige wool dress and hat, beige beaver trimmed coat and brown lizard accessories, dashed away on her wedding trip. Green cymbidium orchids were pinned to her shoulder.

Candles lighted the Piedmont Community church on Saturday night, when at an 8 o'clock service read by Dr. Holland F. Burr, Laura Hunt changed her name to Mrs. Edward Strong. Chrysanthemum and larkspur combined with magnolia leaves, were arranged at the altar when C. Perry Hunt walked with his daughter down the taper-lighted aisle. Rose point lace, originally belonging to the bridegroom's great-great grandmother, formed the headpiece that held her fingertip illusion veil. Laura's flowers were lilies of the valley and white cattelya orchids. Her sister, Mrs. Karl F. Dittman, shared honor place with Dorothy Williams, and Mrs. Ted Hunt was the secondary attendant. Their handsome dresses were of gold tapestry brocade made with bertha collars in an off-the-shoulder effect, and panel skirts. Their bouquets ranges in shade from bronze to pale gold and they wore the same flowers in their hair. Best man for his brother was Addison Strong Jr., and the ushers were Ward Madeira, George Parrish, Russell Pearce and Sam Shannon. After the ceremony, the newly weds received the felicitations of their friends at the Claremont Country Club. From there they left for a wedding trip to the Ojai valley and upon their return will make their home in Berkeley. The gown chosen by Mrs. Hunt for the wedding of her daughter was a full-length sapphire blue chantilly lace with which she wore a matching maline and velvet hat. The senior Mrs. Strong chose a cyclamen lace gown for the occasion. Her matching maline hat was accented by ostrich tips. Both had cattelya orchids.

Their distinguishing detail was in the bouquets, fashioned of graduated colors of chrysanthemums in coppery tones, and matching the flowers they wore in their hair. The bride's bouquet was of lilies of the valley and white cattleya orchids. The couple later left on their wedding trip to San Ysidro ranch in Ojai Valley, and will return to an apartment they have taken in Berkeley. The bride, daughter of the Hunts of Highland Avenue, Piedmont studied at Anna Head's School and the University of California where she was an Alpha Delta Pi.   13. [S541] Obituary of Laura Hunt Strong. San Francisco Chronicle, page D - 99. STRONG, Laura Hunt -- Our wonderful wife, mother and grandmother passed away on Nov. 5, 2000. Laura was born in Oakland, April 10, 1920, and was the daughter of C.P. and Gertrude Hunt. She was married on Nov 5, 1949 to Edward Franklin Strong and they shared a loving marriage for 51 years. She is also survived by her daughters, Sherry Westernoff and Susie Strong; her son Ken Strong; grandchildren, Casey and Michael Strong; son-in law Bill Westernoff; and daughter-in-law Rita Gilmore. Laura attended Anna Head School and the University of California, Berkeley, where she was a member of the Alpha Delta Pi sorority. She was a longtime member of Orinda Country Club, the Estates Investment Club and active with the Oakland Museum White Elephant Sale for many years. Laura loved a good game of golf or bridge, was an avid reader and baked the best pie in Piedmont. The things her family will miss most was her loving interest and constant encouragement, her willingness to listen and her ability to give wise advise, her strong will to live, her independent spirit and the dignity and grace with which she lived her life as fully as possible. At Laura's request, there will be no memorial service. Friends and family wishing to honor her memory are invited to make contributions to the Visiting Nurse Association of Northern California, 1900 Powell St., Ste. 300, Emeryville 94608 or Alta Bates Foundation, 2450 Ashby Ave., Berkeley, 94705.
