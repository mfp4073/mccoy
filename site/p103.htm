  8. [S71] Obituary of William Peter Buck. William Peter Buck was born in Ottensen, Germany, Feb. 2, 1854, and departed this life at his home near Havelock, Iowa, Dec. 18, 1919, at the age of sixty-five years, ten months and sixteen days. Mr. Buck came to this country in September, 1882, and settled in Kankakee county, Illinois, where he made his home for twenty years. He moved to Pocahontas county, Iowa, in 1902. In Dec. 13, 1874, he was united in marriage to Minnie Thon, and to this union eight children were born, one passing away in infancy. He leaves to mourn his passing his wife and seven children, two daughters, Mrs. M. G. Herscher and Mrs. Geo. Miller, and five sons, William and Henry of Havelock, and Louie, Fred and Richard, all of whom live near Rolfe. Mr. Buck was a kind and loving husband and father, and a friend to all. Funeral services were held from the M. E. church Saturday afternoon, Dec. 20, at two o'clock, Rev. C. A. Bates officiating. Interment was made in Powhatan cemetery, near Plover. The bereaved relatives and friends have the sincere sympathy of all in their sorrow.
  9. [S398] Obituary of William Peter Buck. William Peter Buck was born in Ottensen, Germany, Feb. 2, 1854, and passed away at his home at Havelock, December 18, 1919, being 65 years, 10 months and 16 days of age. He came from Germany in September, 1882, and resided in Kankakee county, Illinois, for 20 years and moved from there to Pocahontas county, Iowa, in the spring of 1902. He was united in marriage to Minnie Thon, Dec. 13, 1874, to this union eight children were born, one having died in infancy. He leaves to mourn his loss his wife and seven children, two daughters, Mrs. Geo. Miller and Mrs. M. G. Herscher, William and Henry of Havelock, Fred, Louie and Richard of Rolfe. The funeral was conducted at the Methodist church, Saturday at 1 o'clock, Rec. C.A. Bates, officiating. Burial in a cemetery near Plover. Mr. Buck was a kind and loving father and husband, a good neighbor. We extend our heartfelt sympathy to the bereaved ones from whose fireside the light has fled. We desire to take this means of extending our heartfelt thanks to those who so kindly assisted us during our dark hour of sorrow. Mrs. W. T. Buck and children.

Minnie Thon was born in Hummerson Germany, on the eight day of October 1852, and died on the ninth day of June, 1927, at 3:45 a.m. in Havelock, Iowa, being at the time of her going 74 years, 8 months and one day of age. She was confirmed in the German Lutheran Church when she was 14 years of age, and was married to William P. Buck, December 13, 1874 and to this union was born eight children, six sons and two daughters, all of which survive except the first, who died in infancy. The surviving children are as follows: Mrs. Martha Herscher, Mrs. George Miller and Richard F. and Louis E. of Rolfe, Iowa; William H. and Henry W., of Havelock, Iowa, and Fred J., of Alpha, Minnesota. Mr. Buck, her husband, preceded her in death on the eighteenth day of December, 1919, at the age of 66 years. In the fall of 1882 they moved from Germany to America and lived in the State of Illinois at Kankakee county for 19 years and from there they moved to Pocahontas county in the spring of 1902 near Rolfe, Iowa, where they lived on their farm for nine years and from there they moved to Havelock, when they lived up to the time of their deaths. Mrs. Buck was a kind and loving mother and sacrificed for her children whenever it was necessary and she will be sorely missed by the remaining children and by her other relatives and friends. Following a brief service of reading and prayer at the home in Havelock, Saturday, June 11, at 2:15 o'clock the body was borne across the street to the Methodist church from which the funeral services were conducted by Rev. S. T. Grove of Plover, and Rev. Wilson Gow, of Havelock. Interment was in the Powhattan cemetery, near Plover where Mr. Buck was buried. The five sons, Richard, Louis, William, Henry, Fred and grandson LeRoy Miller served as pall bearers and a large number of friends and relations attended.
