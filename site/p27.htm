George Francis Abseck

M, b. 10 May 1919, d. 13 May 1982
     George Francis Abseck was born on 10 May 1919 at Calumet, Keweenaw Co., Michigan. He was the son of John Lewis Abseck and Helen Mary Stukel. George Francis Abseck married Alice Juanita Bowie, daughter of John Stewart Bowie and Blanche Claudian Mulholland, on 17 September 1949 at Hotchkiss, Delta Co., Colorado.1 George Francis Abseck died on 13 May 1982 at Paonia, Delta Co., Colorado. He was buried at Cedar Hill Cemetery, Paonia, Delta Co., Colorado.

Children of George Francis Abseck and Alice Juanita Bowie

  • Christopher Abseck b. 19 Jun 1952, d. 1 May 1971
  • Patricia Marie Abseck+ b. 17 Mar 1955

Citations

  1. [S142] Wedding Announcement for Alice Juanita Bowie. YOUNG BUSINESSMAN WINS BRIDE FROM BOWIE HOME A double-ring ceremony at which the Rev. Francis J. Wagner officiated in St. Margaret Mary's Catholic church at Hotchkiss at ten-thirty o'clock Saturday morning Sept. 17, united in marriage Miss Alice Juanita Bowie and George Francis Abseck. Attended by her sister, Mrs. Agnes Guthrie, the bride was attired in a street-length gown of haze blue with black accessories and carried a white prayer book with gardenia. Mrs. Guthrie wore a dress of dusty pick with black accessories and her flower was white carnation. Mr. Abseck's brother, J. F. Abseck, was groomsman, and Robert Brinton of Hotchkiss served as usher. Immediately after the ceremony a reception was held in the attractive home at Lazear which had been prepared for the couple's occupancy. Later, the bridal pair departed on a week's honeymoon trip to Electra Lake and Denver. Mrs. George Abseck, younger daughter of Mr. and Mrs. John S. Bowie of Bowie, followed her graduation from high school at Paonia with study in the University of Colorado and seventeen months of service with the Waves in the naval hospital at Quantico, Va. Returning to her home, she assisted her father, general manager of the Juanita Coal and Coke company, with office detail. Mr. Abseck, son of Mr. and Mrs. J. L. Abseck of Pitkin mesa, also is a graduate of Paonia's high school and attended Mesa college at Grand Junction. He was trained as an aircraft technician in California and was engaged in that work in the African campaign of the second World war. As a licensed pilot he saw action later in the Pacific zone. When the A. & M. Electric store at Paonia established its branch at Hotchkiss he was placed in charge and has developed the business into one of the foremost establishments of its kind in western Colorado.
