  1. [S139] Obituary of Russell Jackson Brown. Dr. Russell J. Brown, born in Macon, Missouri on April 19, 1891, died December 21, 1987 in Fullerton, California four months before his 97th birthday. Russell's father, Dr. Jesse J. Brown, moved his dental practice from Hamilton to Macon in 1890. He was a musician and developed Macon's first marching band, giving lessons to young people on many instruments. His wife, Rilla, was a pianist. In the early 1900's, they built the large house on the corner of Ruby and Union. Russell was also a musician and singer. He mastered all brass and reed instruments and after high school graduation he began a lyceum career as a bass singer and trombone player with the Stratfords, a male quartet based in Chicago. He traveled extensively with them for several years and met Winifred ("Peggy") Sheldon, also a professional singer and musician, in Chicago. They were married in 1915 at Peggy's home in Pennsylvania. The couple went to Denver, where Russell attended dental college and practiced dentistry for 48 years. He was in the fourth generation of his family in this profession but his first love was always music. On a miniature spinet piano in the rear of his dental office, he composed many sacred and popular songs. Some were published and are still being played today. One of his best known works is the music written for the Twenty-Third Psalm. Russell and Peggy carried on their musical careers in Denver. He wrote, composed, or arranged music and directed glee clubs and church choirs, while she continued as a popular vocalist. In 1967, after Russell had retired from dentistry, they moved to Oceana, near Oceanside, California, and continued their music until they were 90 years old. Peggy died January 26, 1988 - only a month after Russell. She too would have been 97 years old in April. She and Russell had two children, four grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren. There are buried in Chatsworth, California. - Written by Arleen Brown.
