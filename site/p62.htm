Lewis Main, 76, a pioneer resident of the Colfax and Prairie City community, passed away on Monday of last week at the home of his son Clarence following a short illness. Mr. Main was a farmer for many years, living south of Colfax, but in recent years had retired from the farm and made his home with his son Clarence. Lewis Main, son of Carry Allen and Matilda Main, was born in Hillsboro, Ohio, Nov. 3, 1858, and departed this life from the home of his son, Clarence Main, in Colfax, Iowa, Sept. 9, 1935, aged seventy-six years, eleven months and six days. His early boyhood days were spent in Ohio, but he came to Iowa when a boy of nine years with his parents and settled on a farm near Red Rock. He was united in marriage to Mary E. Gilbert of Prairie City, Iowa, Nov. 27, 1882, who preceded her husband in death Jan. 18, 1898, thirty-seven years ago. To this union were born seven children, four girls and three boys. One daughter, Mrs. Mamie McLaughlin, preceded her father in death. Surviving are three daughters, Mrs. George Kaldenberg of Spring Green, Wis., Mrs. William R. Ward of Des Moines, and Mrs. Roy Cross of Colfax; three sons; Emory of Grand Meadows, Minn., Louis and Clarence of Colfax. He is also survived by one brother John and one sister, Mrs. Ella Cosner of Washington State, twenty-seven grandchildren, twelve great-grandchildren and a host of friends. Mr. Main united with the M. E. church of Prairie City in Dec. 1894. He was a member of the Independent Order of Odd Fellows in that same city. Funeral services were held on Wednesday afternoon of last week from the Methodist church of Colfax with Rev. L. C. McDonald officiating. Music was furnished by Mr. Earl Plummer, Mrs. E. B. Schisle, Gomer Williams and B. F. Van Dyke, who sang two favorite hymns, and Gomer Williams sang a solo. Mrs. Cleo Wall presided at the piano. Pallbearers were E. A. Wheeler. Emory Pflaum, Oliver Pflaum, Geo. McCullough, Fred Boatwright and Charley Holdefer. Burial was made at Prairie City besides his wife.
