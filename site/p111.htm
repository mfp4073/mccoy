Leslie Reed Ward, 52, died Sunday morning at the Veterans Hospital in Des Moines of a heart condition. He had been in failing health the past two years and had been in the hospital ten days at the time of his death. The son of Mr. & Mrs. William Ward he was born 4 Nov. 1910 at Colfax. He attended school at Runnells and was a graduate of the Runnells High School. Before coming to Colfax he had resided several years in Des Moines. Mr. Ward was a member of the Colfax Methodist Church and the American Legion. He served with the Armed Forces during World War Two. He was married to Alice Grosvenor at Colfax Nov. 23, 1946. Mr. Ward was a talented interior decorator and operated his own business in Colfax. Those surviving are his wife Alice, 5 sons, Larry R. of Des Moines, Richard, William, Robert and James at home, 2 daughters, Mrs. Marlene Dray of San Diego, Calif. & Margaret at home, his mother Mrs. William Ward of Altoona, a brother Vinton Ward of Mitchellville, two sisters, Mrs. Helen Painter & Mrs. Helen Cheney of St. Petersburg, Fla. and 5 grandchildren. Funeral services were held at 2 p.m. at the Walters Funeral home in Colfax. Rev. Dale Nicholson, minister of the Methodist Church, conducted the services. Mrs. Frank Evans was organist accompanied by soloist Pat Bone. Bearers were Carroll Action, Carl Elliot, Joe Gannon, Russel Kintz, William Schlosser and George Wall. Military Rites were by Jones Willis Post No. 175 of Colfax.
