Robert Cross and Dale Main were riding on a motorcycle when struck Wednesday night. Robert Cross of this City died Friday morning from injuries received in auto accident on Wednesday evening on the highway east of Prairie City. Robert with Dale Main was riding on Mains motorcycle on the highway about a mile east of Prairie City when they were struck by Merlen Cowman of Prairie City. The boys on the motorcycle had taken Leonard Trunnell out to his sisters and had turned around and started back, when the accident happened. Mr. Cowman claims he was blinded by the lights of another car and did not see the boys's until too late. Both boys were picked up and brought to the Colfax sanatorium where it was discovered Robert had a compound fracture of the leg and internal injuries which caused his death Friday morning. Dale had his neck twisted badly and one leg strained but not serious and is getting about now the same as usual. The motorcycle the boys were riding was owned by and driven by Mr. Main and the fact that Robert was riding behind him is probably the cause of him getting the main blow. Robert was the son of Mr. & Mrs. Royal Cross of this city. He graduated from the Colfax High School with the Class of 1931 and had since been working with his father. The unusually large number of friends who attended Sunday afternoon were evidence of the high esteem in which he was held. Funeral service was held Sunday afternoon 2 p.m. at the Christian Church with Rev. J.W. Tanzior officiating. Honorary pall bearers were Dale Main & Donald Laundeen. The regular pall bearers were Arthur Roberts, John William Jones, George Grundly of Colfax, John Jones of Newton, Vinton Ward of Mitchellville & Reed Ward of Des Moines. Robert Harold Cross was born Jan 5, 1912 in Colfax & came to bless the home of Mr. & Mrs. Roy Cross and one sister, Fern. To meet the loss of the earth are father and mother, one sister Fern, age 20, two brothers Royal age 14 & Delbert age 10, other relatives and many friends.

  8. [S503] Elizabeth Hunt Dittman. Elizabeth Hunt Dittmann died in her sleep Aug. 10 at her home in Piedmont. Born in Spokane, Wash. in 1908, Mrs. Dittmann spent her childhood on the family farm in northeastern Washington. After moving to California her family eventually settled in Oakland, where her father, C.P. Hunt, founded the C.P. Hunt Company, a wholesale auto accessories business. She graduated from Oakland High School and attended U.C. Berkeley. She took voice lessons and eventually joined the San Francisco Opera Chorus, where she participated in a number of productions of the San Francisco Opera Company. She moved to New York City to expand her singing career, but discontinued lessons due to economic conditions. In 1942 she married Karl Dittmann and returned to Oakland, moving to Piedmont in 1948. She co-founded the Piedmont Cooperative Playschool. Her interest in opera never ceased, and she was active in the Opera League for many years. In later years she and her husband were active members of the Home Club. She and her husband Karl raised two sons, Bill Dittmann, now of San Rafael, and Bob Dittmann of Piedmont. She is also survived by her brother, Ted Hunt of Piedmont, and her sister, Laura Strong of Oakland. Burial arrangements were handled by the Neptune Society. Donations may be made in her memory to the Lincoln Child Center, 4368 Lincoln Ave., Oakland 94602.

  6. [S508] Wedding Announcement for Commodore Hunt and Gertrude Teel Michaelis. Whitman College, School Romance Leads to Alter. Two Well Known Walla Walla Students to be Married Today. Wedding of Commodore Hunt and Miss Michaelis to be Solomnized at Spokane. At the home of the bride's parents in Spokane today, will be said the words which will unite in matrimony two young people well known in Walla Walla, Commodore Hunt and Gertrude Teel Michaelis. Mr. Hunt has lived in Walla Walla for several years and has attended the local high school, being prominent in various student activities. In debates, declamation contest, and track meets he has helped to uphold the honor of his school, and has made a host of friends both in the hight school and in the city. Miss Michaelis, whose home is in Spokane, has attended Whitman college for three years, making numerous friends during the months which she has spent here. Had she returned to college this fall she would have entered the senior class and graduated next June. During her college course she was a member of the girls' glee club and was prominent in literary society work, being chosen last year to represent her society, the Libethrean in an inter society debate. Her classmates at Whitman, although they regret to lose her from their midst, will rejoice that there has been such a happy culmination of the acquaintance which began during the school days of both the contracting parties. After their marriage Mr. and Mrs. Hunt plan to make their home in the Colville valley.
  7. [S509] Wedding Announcement of Commodore Hunt and Gertrude Michaelis. Quit School and Marry. Whitman Students Take Up New and Interesting Studies. Miss Gertrude Michaelis of Spokane and Commodore Hunt, formally of this city were united in marriage this afternoon at the home of the bride's's parents in Spokane. Both young people are well known here. Commodore Hunt was formerly a resident of the city. He was prominent in literary and athletic circles at the high school. He was a member of several debating teams and several times represented the school in declamation contests. In athletics he was one of the high school's track men and football players and was well known in both gridiron and oval. Mr. Hunt has many friends in the city who will be glad to learn of the happy marriage. Miss Michaelis, although her home is in Spokane, is well known here, especially in college circles. She has attended Whitman for three years and would have graduated this year had not the charms of married life overcome those of the pursuit of knowledge. Miss Michaelis was one of the best known members of her literary society and was a member of the girl's glee club. The wedding is the culmination of a romance begun three years ago when the two met at a reception. They will make their home in the Colville valley, where the groom has business interests.

  10. [S540] Obituary of Gertrude Hunt. Oakland Tribune, page 20. Services for Mrs. Hunt Set Tuesday. PIEDMONT -- Memorial services will be held Tuesday for Mrs. Gertrude M. Hunt, widow of the late Commodore Perry Hunt, who died Saturday afternoon of a heart attack. She was 80. Mr. Hunt established the C.P. Hunt Co., an Oakland automotive accessories firm, after the couple came here in 1920 from Ohio. He was widely known in Northern California automobile circles. Mrs. Hunt is survived by a son, Theodore, and two daughters, Mrs. Karl F. Dittmann and Mrs. Edward F. Strong, all of Piedmont. Other survivors include seven grandchildren and two great-grandchildren. Services will be held at 11 a.m. in the Piedmont Community Church, 400 Highland Ave.
