Lee, M.D. the well known homoeopathist, took place yesterday afternoon, from his residence southwest corner of Thirty-eighth and Chestnut streets, the services at the house being attended by a large number of his friends. The house was crowed, and a hundred or more persons stood on the porch and in the front garden in the cold rain during the entire ceremony. The religious exercises were conducted by the Rev. Dr. W.F. Watkins, Rector of the Protestant Episcopal Church of the Saviour; the Rev. Dr. J. Addison Henry, of the Princeton Presbyterian Church, and the Rev. Joseph Welch, of the Asbury Methodist Episcopal Church. The remains were interred at Woodlands Cemetery, the pall-bearers being Ex. Governor Hoyt, Ex-Governor Pattison, Dr. Thomas G. Marton. John Scott, M.P. Stacy, Samuel Field, Dr. A.K. Thomas, Dr. George I. McLeod, Henry M. Dechert, Caleb J. Milne, Cadwalader Biddle and Amos Bonsall.
    The members of the Twenty-seventh Section School Board and Hamilton Lodge, No. 274, of Free and Acepted Masons, attended the funeral. There were present, among many others, Judge Allison, Mr. Justice Sterritt, the Rev. Dr. W.F. Paddock, the Rev. Dr. Henry C. McCook, the Rev. Robert F. Innes, Dr. Andrew J. Ourt, Edward W. Patton, Henry W. Halliwell, Samuel L. Smedley, W,. Dixey, Peter Rose and Dr. James Truman.
