William Henry Elwood

M, b. 1866, d. 1961
     William Henry Elwood was born in 1866 at Monroe, Jasper Co., Iowa.1 He was the son of Dr. Azariah Sinclair Elwood and Mary Elizabeth Howard. William Henry Elwood died in 1961. He was buried at Ceres Cemetery, Ceres, Stanislaus Co., California. Note.2

Citations

  1. [S354] Genealogy of Arthur Elwood, page 6.
  2. [S377] Letter from Teri Johnson to Mary Lou Marshall dated 31 May 2002, His name was William Henry Elwood, and he was Azariah Sinclair Elwood and Mary Elizabeth (Howard) Elwood's son. He was one of 3 children, born in 1866, in Monroe, Iowa. He left Colorado where his family was later located, and was in the Ceres/Modesto, California area by about 1913. He met my grandfather sometime between then and 1931 when he started working for him. My grandfather was a poultry man, and William lived on the ranch property. My grandparents and Willaim became great friends, and the two times they moved, he moved right along with them. Both times he drove a loaded wagon to the new location. The last being on the corner of Crowslanding Road and Whitmore Road, in South Modesto, where he spent the last 22 years of his life. He was there for my father's childhood and good part of mine. We all lived on the property of the poultry ranch (Sanders Hatchery), so were very close. When he died in 1961, it was my family that saw to his burial. He is buried in the Ceres Cemetery, Ceres, California. He left the things he had to my grandparents, and that's how I came to have all these great pictures, etc. Before William left Colorado he was a miner and drove stage coach out of Cripple Creek. "Uncle Will" always said he had no relatives, so it's been a labor of love discovering the relatives he didn't think he had, and sharing with the Elwood ancestors, all of these great things. (E-mail to Mary Lou Marxhall, who forwarded it to James Benham, 31 May 2002.
