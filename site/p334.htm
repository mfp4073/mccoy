  2. [S773] Obituary of Danny Lee Schnell. Williams-Grand Canyon News, Page 5A, Danny Lee Schnell, (January 29, 1933--August 8, 2018) 83, of Scottsdale, Arizona passed away comfortably in his home on August 8, 2018.
    Danny was born in Boulder, Colorado and raised in Williams, Arizona. He was a wonderful, loving husband, brother, father, and grandfather.
    Danny is survived by his wife of 62 years, Alice (Burrage), sister Sandra (Willsey), four daughters Stephanie (Marty) Johnson, Kelly (Bill) Weaver, Sherrylee (Gary) Price, and Tracy Foreman, and eight grandchildren. He was preceded in death by his parents, Montyne and Henry Benham. He was a star basketball player at Williams High School in Williams before graduating in 1951. After graduating high school, he served in the Navy during the Korean War. Danny attended New Mexico State University in Las Cruces, where he earned a bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering and later went on to work for Motorola for 30 years before retiring in 1993. After retiring, be became a master gardener volunteer with the Maricopa County extension service. In addition to being a master gardener, Danny loved to spend his time camping, golfing, fishing, hunting and volunteering at the Desert Botanical Garden in Phoenix.
    A Memorial Service was held at Messinger Pinnacle Peak Mortuary Aug. 11 at 11:00 a.m. In lieu of flowers, donations can be made to the Maricopa County Master Gardener program.

  3. [S498] Obituary for Nancy Carter. Williams--Grand Canyon News, Nancy Carter, 65, of Williams, passed away on Sunday, July 25, 2010. She was born on June 28, 1945 to Paul and Elizabeth Morel. Nancy was an avid hunter and fisherman and seemed to always take "wall hangers," but was a meat hunter first. She was the first one out there skinning, field dressing and processing all animals from big game, down to skunks off trap lines. Raccons and skunks were never safe around her chicken coop. She enjoyed fly fishing in the High Sierras of California. Nancy was a founding member of the Williams Animal Action Group (WAAG), and served on the board of directors as Treasurer and Vice President, along with organizing annual vaccination clinics. Nancy was extremely talented when it came to gardening. She had that "green thumb" and won the most blue ribbons at the Coconino County Fair five years in a row. She loved raising and showing rabbits, Flemish Giants were her favorite. Nancy documented all inventory on the Williams and Ash Fork cemeteries along with a few outlying areas. She also compiled two Forest Service cookbooks, which were a big hit with the locals. Nancy was also very involved with photography, and also with genealogy. At times whe even developed her own film. If you asked her to help you with either, she was more than happy to. Nancy had a deep love for her family and animals and it showed in her thousands of photos taken. Nancy is survived by her husband of 42 years Thomas Carter, her son P.T. (Jennifer) Carter, her sister Susie (Bill) Baer and her grandchildren Mckenzie and Cheznie Carter. Services will be held at the Williams Cemetery on Saturday, July 31, 2010 at 10 a.m. A celebration of life will be held at 11 a.m. aat the American Legion Hut at 425 W. Grant Ave., in Williams. In lieu of flowers, please make a donation to WAAG, P.O. Box 23, Williams, AZ 86046 or contact Kili Kaliche at (928) 635-2595.
