    1. In the first world war, he was gassed once. He also had a tree fall on him when it was blown up by a shell. I am trying to get a copy of his medical records to verify this, because his discharge papers, under "Wounds received in service" say "None."
    2. He drove a 20 mule team in the wheat fields of Idaho when a younger man. They lived in Weiser, Idaho for a number of years, and he entered the service from Boise, Idaho.
    3. He was a very strong man (for a short man of 5'-8" tall). One time he was in the barn stall with one of the mules, and it kicked him so that he flew out the "mucking" hole into the pile of manure outside. It got him so mad that he went back in and picked up a harness strap in both hands. He came up behind the mule, raised the strap up over his head with his hands apart and slapped it down over the mules rear, driving it to its knees. Apparently, that mule -- which had been known for its cantankerousness -- never misbehaved with him again.
