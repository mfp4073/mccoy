  2. [S565] Source not known, Daniel Bradish, b 1761 was a Revolutionary War pensioner. He was a private and "Minute Man" in the Massachusetts troops under Colonels Whitney, Cushing and Denny. He was born in Westborough, Mass. and died in Potsdam, New York. D.A.R. records indicate he was married to Phoebe Jones in 1785. Census records indicate that he maintained a family in Wethersfield, Windsor County, Vermont in 1800 prior to coming to Northern New York. His birth is recorded as April 30, 1760 and death as November 4, 1855.
    History of Revolutionary War lists Daniel Bradish's service as follows: Private in Capt. David Brent's Co.; Col. Job Cushing's regiment; enlisted Sept. 5, 1777; discharged Nov. 29, 1777; service 3 months 6 days at the Northward. Daniel Bradish of Winchendon, private Capt. William Henry's Co.; Col. Whitney's regiment; enlisted May 5, 1777; discharged July 5, 1777; service 2 months 10 days at Rhode Island, enlistment 2 months. Roll sworn to at Boston. Additional notes suggest he born April 30, 1760.
