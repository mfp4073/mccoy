Cheryl Lynn Hill, the daughter of Norman and Helen Hill of Runnells, Iowa, became the bride of Bruce Allen Gilbert, son of Marvin and Mildred Gilbert of Prairie City, Iowa, on Saturday, June 9, at 5:30 p.m. The candlelight, double-ring wedding ceremony was held in the Christian Church of Prairie City. Officiating at the service were Pastor Marshall Myers of the Christian Church of Prairie City, and Pastor Tom Mabe of the First Baptist church in Colfax. The bride, given in marriage by her father and mother, wore a white satin gown featuring a sweetheart neckline and a sequined bodice of crystal organza. The short ruffled sleeves, bodice and high collar were accented by eye-catching crystals. Strands of pearl beads adorned the keyhole back of the fitted bodice. The flared skirt was embellished by rows of organza ruffles flowing to a chapel-length train. Her headpiece was a wreath of flowers and pearl beads. Her ensemble was completed by white satin gloves. The bride carried a cascading bouquet of pink-tipped roses and white carnations. Linda McGhee of Altoona, sister of the bride, was matron of honor and Bobbi Streeter of Prairie City, friend of the bride and groom, was bridesmaid. Dan Adams of Prairie City, friend of the groom, served as best man and Mike Streeter, also of Prairie City and friend of the bride and groom, was groomsman. Amanda Gilbert and Aaron Gilbert, niece and nephew of the groom, were flower girl and ring bearer. The bride's attendants wore burgundy satin, tea-length dresses and carried pink sweet-heart roses. The flower girl was attired in a pink satin tea-length dress. The attendants wore lace gauntlets. The groom wore a white tuxedo with tails. The groomsmen wore silver tuxedos with silver bow ties. The guests were seated by Scott Hill, brother of the bride, and Mike Gilbert, brother of the groom. Special music was provided by Nancy Garwick, soloist, who sang "The Wedding Song," "Time in a Bottle," and "Through the Eyes of Love." She was accompanied at the piano by Barb Steenhoek. The guest book attendant was Melissa Wemark. The host for the evening was David McGhee, brother-in-law of the bride, and the hostess was Sheryl Hill, sister-in-law of the bride. Following the ceremony, a reception was held in the basement of the church. Cake, coffee, and punch were served by Sharon Gilbert, Terri Gilbert, Brenda Van Ryswyk, and Melinda Schafman. Kayla Hill and Connie Terri, cousins of the bride, attended the gift table. The couple honeymooned in Vero Beach, Florida, and now reside in Runnells. He is employed by R & R Welding in Des Moines. Mrs. Gilbert is a nurse at Mercy Medical Center in Des Moines. A wedding rehearsal dinner was hosted by the groom's parents, Marvin and Mildred Gilbert, at the Prairie City Community Center on Friday, Jun 8.

George Garlan Sanders

M, b. 18 August 1883, d. 10 April 1936
George Garlan Sanders
     George Garlan Sanders was born on 18 August 1883 at Carlisle, Warren Co., Iowa.1 He was the son of David Franklin Sanders and Mary Elizabeth Owens. George Garlan Sanders was born on 15 October 1883 at Carlisle, Warren Co., Iowa.2 He married Jennie Dell O'Neil, daughter of George W. O'Neill and Adelphia Goowin, on 26 December 1906 at Ft. Pierre, Stanley Co., South Dakota.2 George Garlan Sanders married Grace Chevalier in November 1927. George Garlan Sanders died on 10 April 1936 at Chandler, Maricopa Co., Arizona.2,3 Obituary.4 He was buried on 12 April 1936 at Carlisle Cemetery, Carlisle, Warren Co., Iowa.1,5 Note.6

Child of George Garlan Sanders and Jennie Dell O'Neil

  4. [S617] Obituary for George Garlan Sanders. George Garlan Sanders was born in Carlisle, Iowa August 18, 1883. He died at his home in Chandler, Arizona April 10, 1936. His boyhood was spent in the place of his birth. In his early twenties he moved to Ft. Pierre, South Dakota where he married Jennie Dell O'Neil to which union was born his only child Francis G. Sanders. His wife died 3 years after their marriage at Carlisle, Iowa. In 1917 he came to Chandler, Arizona to engage in cotton raising. In 1920 he moved to Marana, Arizona, where he engaged in the same business clearing his land from the virgin desert. In November 1927 he was married to Grace Chavalier of Des Moines, Iowa. Three years ago ill health compelled him to move back to Chandler where he resided until his death. His friends recognized him as a keen businessman and a good neighbor. He is survived by his wife, son and four sisters; Mrs. Kramer M. Gilbert of Chandler, Arizona, Mrs. Carl Kail of Lincoln, Nebraska, Mrs. G.D. Schooler of Hartford, Iowa and Mrs. H.J. Wright of Carlisle, Iowa.

  6. [S565] Source not known, Jennie died on my father's 2nd birthday from Diphtheria. They were moving from Pierre, South Dakota to I'm not for sure where. They stopped off in Carlisle, Iowa, to visit some of my Grandfather Sanders relatives, when she came down with diphtheria. My father also had diphtheria. Uncle Kramer Gilbert was already a doctor, and he could get hold of some medicine they could not get in Carlisle. Kramer sent the medicine to Carlisle, but it was too late for Jennie. Dad was raised by Aunt Bessie, Aunt Bulla, Aunt Marion (Harriet Robins Troxill's mother). He stayed six months with each. Garlan Sanders moved to Marana, Arizona where he homesteaded land. A cotton company bought out the Marana farm, and he moved to Chandler, Arizona, where he bought the ranch I grew up on. Garlan needed a wife, so around the time my father was in, I think, his late teens, a friend made arrangements for Grace, by then an "old maid" to come West, as kind of an "mail order bride," arranged by friends. Garlan lived only six years after marring Grace. (Written by Evelyn Sanders Manley, Fall 1999).
