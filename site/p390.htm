  2. [S522] Wedding Announcement of Grace Groff. The Union Register, Grace Groff Chooses White Satin for Wedding Saturday. Wearing an ivory satin directoire gown with a long court train, trimmed with family rose-paint lace, and carrying an arm bouquet of gardenias and Johanna Hill roses, Miss Grace Groff, daughter of Mr. and Mrs. John David Groff, of Columbus, became the bride of Howard VanAtta, son of Mr. and Mrs. Ray Hull VanAtta, of Rye Beach. The ceremony was performed Saturday afternoon, in the Grace Methodist Protestant church, with Rev. Frank Lawrence Brown officiating. While the guests were assembling, Mrs. Frank Brown played a program of music and Mr. Robert Cockerell sang. The bride entered the church with her father. Mrs. Harold Nelson of Cincinnati, was matron of honor and Miss Mary Marguerite Gaal, 175 Deshler avenue, Columbus, was maid of honor. Their gowns were in autumn shades of green and rust taffeta fashioned along directoire lines. The full shirts fell in slight trains and they wore velvet bonnets in colors matching their gowns and carried bronze anemones and chrysanthemums. Mr. John A. Rousculp, brother-in-law of the bride, was best man. The ushers were Mr. Lawrence E. Taber, Mr. Walter L. Virtue, Mr. Cassius H. Makeever, and Mr. Frederick W. Wieland, all of Mt. Gilead. For her daughter's wedding Mrs. Groff chose a dress of royal blue velvet and wore a corsage of roses. Mrs. Rousculp, sister of the bride, wore a wine colored velvet dress and a corsage of yellow roses, while Mrs. VanAtta, mother of the bridegroom, was dressed in a brown crepe dress and wore a corsage of roses also. Following the ceremony a reception was held at the bride's home. After the middle of October, the couple will reside at 135 West High street, Mt. Gilead. The bride was graduated from Ohio State university and is affiliated with Alpha Sigma Alpha sorority. She has been teaching in the Alliance high school but was formerly a teacher of Home Economics in Mt. Gilead high school. Mr. VanAtta attended Ohio State university and is affiliated with the State Highway department at the Delaware office.

Returned home to Berkeley from a honeymoon in Carmel are Mr. and Mrs. Steven Hunt (Marilyn Bliss) who exchanged vows a fortnight ago at the First Congregational Church of Berkeley, Dr. Vere Loper conducting the rites. Both students at the University of California, Marilyn is the daughter of Mr. and Mrs. Everett L. Bliss of San Juan Ave. in the college city and her husband is the son of Theodore Hunts of Piedmont. The bride asked her sister, Carol Bliss, to be maid of honor and bridesmaids were Betty Bliss, a cousin from San Jose, Betsy Parker and Marilyn Dill, of Berkeley, and two sorority sisters, Janet King and Linda Proctor of Piedmont. All wore frocks of pink organza over taffeta and carried bouquet of pink roses and bouvardia. Standing with the benedict as best man was Martin Ullman of Piedmont, and ushering duties were assigned to Fred Henchell, Jon King, Don Geddes, Clark Crum and Hank Vierregger. The newlyweds received at the Twentieth Century Club.

  6. [S528] Wedding Announcement of Laura Hunt. Laura Hunt Marries Edward Strong In Rites at Piedmont Church. Before an arrangement of chrysanthemums, larkspur and magnolia leaves on the alter of the Piedmont Community Church last Saturday night, Miss Laura Hunt became the bride of Mr. Edward Franklin Strong. C. Perry Hunt escorted his daughter down the taper-lighted aisle and the wedding lines were read by Dr. Holland F. Burr. A princess-styled rose blush pearl satin gown, fashioned with a sweetheart neckline, was worn by the bride. Her Mary Queen of Scots headpiece of heirloom rosepoint lace, originally worn by the groom's maternal great-great-grandmother, held in place her finger tip veil of tulle which was combined with rare lace from her mother's wedding veil. She carried white cattleya orchids and lilies-of-the-valley. In the bridal procession, Mrs. Karl F. Dittman, the bride's sister, as honor matron, Miss Dorothy Williams as honor maid and Mrs. Ted P. Hunt, Laura's sister-in-law, wore identical frocks of gold tapestry brocade and their headdress and bouquets were of chrysanthemums which shaded from gold to bronze. Pearl chokers, gifts of the bride, were worn by each. Addison Strong, J., was best man for his brother, while Ward Madiera, Russell Pearce, George Parrish and Sam Shannon seated the guests. The justweds received the well wishes of their family and friends at the Claremont Country Club. Mrs. Hunt greeted the wedding guests in a full length Chantilly lace gown of sapphire blue. Her hat was of blue maline and velvet ribbon. White orchids completed her ensemble. The groom's mother, Mrs. Addison G. Strong, wore a cyclamen toned creation of Chantilly lace and her matching maline hat was trimmed with tiny ostrich tips. Her corsage was of gold orchids. Mr. and Mrs. Strong are now honeymooning at the San Ysidro Inn in the Ojai Valley. They will make their home in Berkeley. The outstretched hands of Dorothy Williams caught the bridal bouquet, as the new Mrs. Strong, wearing a beige wool dress and hat, beige beaver trimmed coat and brown lizard accessories, dashed away on her wedding trip. Green cymbidium orchids were pinned to her shoulder.
  7. [S529] Wedding Announcement of Laura Hunt. Laura Hunt Was Wed to Edward Strong on Saturday. Candles lighted the Piedmont Community church on Saturday night, when at an 8 o'clock service read by Dr. Holland F. Burr, Laura Hunt changed her name to Mrs. Edward Strong. Chrysanthemum and larkspur combined with magnolia leaves, were arranged at the altar when C. Perry Hunt walked with his daughter down the taper-lighted aisle. Laura's blush-colored satin gown was styled in princess lines. The fitted bodice was made with a sweetheart neckline and long sleeves. Rose point lace, originally belonging to the bridegroom's great-great grandmother, formed the headpiece that held her fingertip illusion veil. Laura's flowers were lilies of the valley and white cattelya orchids. Her sister, Mrs. Karl F. Dittman, shared honor place with Dorothy Williams, and Mrs. Ted Hunt was the secondary attendant. Their handsome dresses were of gold tapestry brocade made with bertha collars in an off-the-shoulder effect, and panel skirts. Their bouquets ranges in shade from bronze to pale gold and they wore the same flowers in their hair. Best man for his brother was Addison Strong Jr., and the ushers were Ward Madeira, George Parrish, Russell Pearce and Sam Shannon. After the ceremony, the newly weds received the felicitations of their friends at the Claremont Country Club. From there they left for a wedding trip to the Ojai valley and upon their return will make their home in Berkeley. The gown chosen by Mrs. Hunt for the wedding of her daughter was a full-length sapphire blue chantilly lace with which she wore a matching maline and velvet hat. The senior Mrs. Strong chose a cyclamen lace gown for the occasion. Her matching maline hat was accented by ostrich tips. Both had cattelya orchids.
  8. [S530] Wedding Announcement for Laura Hunt. Laura Hunt Is Married In Piedmont. Wearing a Mary Queen of Scots headpiece made of heirloom rose-point lace belonging to the benedict's mother, and a wedding gown of rose blush satin made princess style, Laura Hunt, Saturday evening, became Mrs. Edward Franklin Strong at Piedmont Community Church. C. Perry Hunt and his daughter walked up an aisle marked with white candles based in magnolia leaves, to the alter decorated with white chrysanthemums and larkspur. There the bridegroom and his brother, Addison C. Strong Jr. of Berkeley, the best man awaited them. The bridal attendants who marched ahead of Laura and her father were Mrs. Karl F. Dittman, sister of the bride and matron of honor; Dorothy Williams, maid of honor, and Mrs. Ted P. Hunt, sister-in-law of the bride, all of Piedmont. In the usher retinue were Ward Madeira of Berkeley, George Parrish and Russell Pearce of Oakland, and Sam Shannon of Marysville. Identical dresses of gold tapestry satin brocade with bertha collars and full skirts were worn by all the bride's attendants. Each also wore a pearl choker, gift of the bride. Their distinguishing detail was in the bouquets, fashioned of graduated colors of chrysanthemums in coppery tones, and matching the flowers they wore in their hair. The bride's bouquet was of lilies of the valley and white cattleya orchids. Her mother had white orchids with a deep red center to pin on her sapphire blue Chantilly lace, sequin trimmed, with which she wore a matching hat of blue malines and velvet ribbon. Mrs. Addison Strong, who loaned her future daughter-in-law the lace which held the fingertip wedding veil in place, wore a cyclamen colored Chantilly lace gown. Tiny ostrich tips trimmed the matching malines hat, and gold cattleya orchids were chosen for the corsage, to match Mrs. Strong's Beauvals evening bag. Dr. Holland F. Burr officiated at the 8 o'clock nuptials, which were followed by a reception at the Claremont Country Club, where masses of white chrysanthemums were used in decoration. The couple later left on their wedding trip to San Ysidro ranch in Ojai Valley, and will return to an apartment they have taken in Berkeley. The bride, daughter of the Hunts of Highland Avenue, Piedmont studied at Anna Head's School and the University of California where she was an Alpha Delta Pi. Her husband is the son is the son of Mr. and Mrs. Strong of Mendocino Avenue in Berkeley, and was a Delta Tau Delta at the University of California. He later received a degree from the Stanford Graduate School of Business.
